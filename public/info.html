Overview

Mafia is a social deduction game created by Dmitry Davidoff in 1986 modelling a conflict between two groups: an informed minority (the evils), and an uninformed majority (the villagers). At the start of the game, each player is secretly assigned a role affiliated with one of these teams. The game has two alternating phases: first, a night role, during which those with night killing powers may covertly kill other players, and second, a day role, in which surviving players debate the identities of players and vote to eliminate a suspect. The game continues until a faction achieves its win condition; for village, this usually means eliminating all evils, while for evils, this usually means reaching numerical equality with the village.

Roles

Village: The "good" role. The villagers win by eliminating all mafia. Villagers can eliminate mafia members by voting them off during the day cycle. There are 3 villagers per 1 mafia.

Mafia: The "evil" role. The mafia win by reaching equal numbers with the villagers. Mafia can eliminate villagers by killing them off during nighttime, or by manipulating the vote during the day cycle. There is one mafia per 3 villagers.
